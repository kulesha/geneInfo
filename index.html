geneSpy - tool to help in your genome editing work
Authors: Eugene Kulesha , Vera Grinkevich
Are you into genome editing ? Do you need to see a protein with gene sequence, find amino acid by its position in protein, select gene fragment and use it in BLAST sequence similarity search ? geneSpy is here to help.

  1. Choose the source of the data ( top right corner ): By default the tool works with the latest species assemblies used by Ensembl. You can choose an older assembly used in Ensembl GRCh37.
  2. Select the species of interest.
  3. Enter your gene name and click Find.
  4. To see the protein sequence choose a transcript from the dropdown.
  5. Now you can locate an amino acid by its position or,
  6. Find a piece of DNA in the displayed sequence or,
  7. Select a fragment of DNA using your mouse ( you can go across multiple lines ) and send it to the BLAST tool.
